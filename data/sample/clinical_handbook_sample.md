## Mood Presentations

### Low Mood

Low mood describes a sustained period of diminished interest and reduced energy.

#### Assessment

For the purposes of this sample, a low-mood presentation is noted when reduced
interest persists across most of the day for an extended span, accompanied by
changes in sleep or appetite and a drop in concentration. The assessor records
onset, duration, and impact on daily functioning.

#### Notes

This sample distinguishes transient sadness from a sustained presentation by
duration and by interference with routine activity.

### Elevated Mood

Elevated mood describes a period of unusually high energy and reduced need for
rest.

#### Assessment

In this sample, an elevated-mood presentation is noted when increased activity
and reduced need for sleep appear together over a defined window, with a marked
change from the person's usual baseline.

## Anxiety Presentations

### Generalized Worry

Generalized worry describes persistent, hard-to-control apprehension across many
areas of life.

#### Assessment

This sample records generalized worry when apprehension is present more days than
not over an extended span and is paired with restlessness, muscle tension, or
disturbed sleep.

### Panic Episodes

A panic episode is an abrupt surge of intense discomfort that peaks within
minutes.

#### Assessment

For this sample, assessing a panic episode means documenting the abrupt onset,
the time to peak, and the physical sensations reported — for example a racing
heartbeat, shortness of breath, or dizziness. The assessor also notes whether
the person changes behavior to avoid future episodes.

#### Notes

This sample separates a single panic episode from a recurrent pattern by
counting episodes over time and by the presence of anticipatory avoidance.

## Sleep Presentations

### Difficulty Initiating Sleep

Difficulty initiating sleep describes a consistent delay between intending to
sleep and falling asleep.

#### Assessment

In this sample, the presentation is noted when sleep onset is delayed on most
nights over an extended span and the person reports daytime consequences such as
fatigue or reduced concentration.
